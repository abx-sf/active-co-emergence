These are not artworks. They are operational fields.

Each field exposes a different condition in which form, relation and instability emerge within a dynamic system.

Rather than representing something, these computational fields allow a process to unfold.

The field does not illustrate theory. It enacts it.

Plural Emergent Field

A distributed field in which multiple elements interact through attraction and local relations. There is no central structure. Order appears only as a temporary emergence.

Continuous Reconfiguration

A distributed structure that continuously reorganizes itself. The field does not stabilize into a defined form. It remains in constant transformation, revealing process rather than representation.

Field Fracture

A structural field exposed to progressive rupture. Local resistance and singularities generate shifts and reconfiguration within the system. Stability becomes temporary and fragile.
