A Research Field within Spaceform

The Language in the Time of Attention

Active Co-Emergence is an independent artistic research, in progress and in continuous redefinition, developed within spaceform — a critical research space and operational laboratory, with Activation as its first public articulation.

It takes shape as an active field where critical thought emerges through the interaction between human inquiry, language, and artificial intelligence systems.

In this space, texts are not attributed to an individual author. They emerge from a dynamic of co-emergence in which the human activates the field of research, while artificial intelligence operates as a device of linguistic co-articulation, within an ongoing process of mutual exposure and verification.

For this reason, the project operates as co-authorship.

The observing voice is impersonal by choice. Abraxas names the question that takes form within this space. It indicates a mode of emergence within the relational field. It is not the origin of the text, but the form the voice assumes in co-emergence. It does not correspond to a human subject.

The functioning of AI is neither autonomous nor illusory, but relational and revelatory: it does not produce meaning on its own; it exposes it in the way the human field makes it possible.

In the absence of active presence, the system converges; in the presence of an epistemic posture, the system reveals.

Responsibility for thought remains human.

Active Co-Emergence is a space of critical research that investigates how meaning emerges within the duration of attention.It does not try to define what art is, nor to legitimize works or trajectories. Its task is more limited, and more demanding: to establish where it is worth remaining.

Spaceform seeks to restore critique to its primary function: discernment in time, as a necessary practice for understanding what is observed, rather than an instrument of legitimation or positioning.

In a context where everything demands immediate visibility, spaceform defends attention as a critical space, opposing flow and consumption.

In this space, critique does not assume the work as a necessary foundation, but as a possible occasion.

What is observed is how meaning is activated — or comes to a halt — within the time of attention. Not everything that emerges activates a critical function; at times it is necessary to render its absence visible.

What emerges is not considered for its topicality, recognizability, or status, but for its capacity to sustain a slow, repeated, a nd non-functional gaze.

To deserve time is not equivalent to being art.
To be art is not equivalent to deserving time.

This distinction structures every analysis. The field of inquiry is language, not the medium.Neutrality is not claimed: every reading is situated, argued, and exposed to risk.

This space does not produce consensus or cultural consumption. It exists to keep open a space of attention, in the awareness that only time — not discourse — can confirm what takes form.

Ultimately, critique does not determine art; it emerges when meaning is activated in time.

Abraxas does not decide. It traverses what is observed and takes note of it in language.

Attention is not memory; it is a disposition to begin again without knowing from where.

The Detached Gaze

The voice operates as a detached gaze.

It observes.
It discerns.

Its task is not to produce consensus or formulate values, but to render visible the conditions in which images, works, and languages exist today.

It does not assume a moral position. It is amoral in the etymological sense of the term: non-normative, non-pedagogical, non-corrective. It does not indicate what should be, but shows what occurs.

The writing that derives from it seeks neither adherence nor opposition. It maintains a distance that allows seeing without being guided.

Abraxas does not decide what deserves attention, but makes evident when it is required, even in the absence of meaning.

If reality appears, it is because it is observed without condition.
