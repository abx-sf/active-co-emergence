The Place of Legitimation

Part of the research sequence — Activation

Something can be taken without passing through what would have made it necessary.

The conditions in which something is taken as valid have changed.

The difference does not concern the nature of the work, but the point at which its validation occurs.

It is not a substitution, but a shift.

Legitimation no longer depends on the work as the place of stabilization. It is not in the work that something consolidates, exposes itself, measures itself against what might not hold.

This point has shifted.

It occurs in its exposure, in its circulation, in the devices that make its visibility and activation immediate. What appears in these spaces can be integrated, recognized, relaunched without referring to further verification.

Under these conditions, context does not legitimate by declaring value.

It selects.

What does not interrupt is taken.

Validation precedes verification.

This does not imply that the distinction between what holds and what does not hold has disappeared. But it does not determine what is taken.

Value does not stabilize in the same way.

The time required for verification no longer coincides with the time of circulation. What is activated can operate immediately, produce effects, modify other things, without its hold being tested.

Under these conditions, the relation between work and recognition misaligns. It is not the work that is recognized. Recognition occurs. This does not make the field indistinct. Not everything is equivalent. But what differs is not necessarily distinguished.

Distinction does not disappear. It is not exercised.

It is in this gap that a particular condition opens. Something can operate as art without it being possible to establish it.

Verification is not denied. It is displaced.

It can occur elsewhere, at a later time, or not occur at all. But it does not condition what, in the present, is taken. Not everything that appears as meaningful is so. But it can operate as such.

This is where the risk opens: not that something is misunderstood, but that it is taken without being passed through. That it holds enough to be relaunched, without what supports it being tested.

We are not facing an excess of words. But a condition in which words can operate without having to hold.

The question is not to immediately distinguish what has value from what does not. But to recognize the conditions in which something passes as such.
