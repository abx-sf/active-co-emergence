Active Co-Emergence — The Language in the Time of Attention

spaceform — Critical Research Space

The Work, in Function

Part of the research sequence — Activation

Critique does not coincide with what comes into speech, nor with the moment in which something is named. Its possibility precedes the need to speak and does not arise from the encounter with a work, nor from the necessity of responding to it. It operates at a point prior to the distinction between object and horizon, between form and context, when what appears has not yet closed into stable evidence and experience cannot yet rely on an immediately available grammar.

Within this space, critique does not interpret, explain, or react. It holds a difference that would otherwise dissolve into the flow of evidence before it can become meaning. The work may occupy this space and render it perceptible, but it does not found it. It is one of the ways through which a regime of visibility becomes readable, not its origin. For this reason, critique does not depend on the work: its activity does not concern what is already isolated as an object, but the very possibility of isolating something without saturating it.

The work, although autonomous in its own language, is not sufficient to itself. Not because it lacks meaning, but because meaning alone does not produce duration. Without this passage, the work remains an event: it appears, circulates, is recognized, yet it does not persist. Critique does not intervene to complete it through explanation, nor to determine its value, but to expose it to a dimension in which it can be thought, reopened, contested. In this sense, critique does not determine the work; it grants it an epistemic structure, that is, the possibility of persisting over time as a shared experience of thought.

This passage does not concern the work as such, but the perceptual horizon that the work activates, modifies, or renders readable. Critique does not position itself before the work as a subject interpreting an object; it situates itself within the configuration itself, intercepting its conditions, its limits, its tensions. What becomes readable is not what the work "says" but the regime of visibility and attention that becomes perceptible through the work.

Critical vision therefore is neither centered nor expressive. It does not coincide with a personal point of view, nor with a subjective position. It does not belong to the critic; it moves through the configuration the critic intercepts. The more autonomous this vision becomes, the more the critic withdraws. The critic’s capacity to see does not consist in adding meanings, but in anticipating configurations of readability, making a reading possible before it stabilizes as interpretation. The critic anticipates reading not in order to guide it, but to prevent it from immediately coinciding with recognition.

The language that emerges from this process therefore does not have an expansive function. It does not amplify, accumulate, or over-clarify. It delimits. When vision is precise, words become necessary in the strictest sense: they serve to activate a structure without colonizing it. Before a work that functions, language contracts, because every additional word risks occupying what should remain traversable.

The critical text is not the vision itself, but a function through which a vision can assume a shareable form without losing its tension.

Within this framework, critique does not derive from the work.

The work is one of the occasions in which critique becomes visible, not its origin. Its autonomy does not coincide with a constant presence: it may remain latent until conditions require its activation.

When it activates, it neither protects nor fixes the work, but exposes it to a duration it does not control.

Without this exposure, the work remains alone with its own language; with it, it enters a dimension in which it can be thought without being closed.

Critique guarantees nothing.

It promises no outcomes and establishes no conclusions.

It makes it possible for something to be held without being immediately consumed.

Within this holding — fragile and reversible — the work can exist not only as appearance, but as an experience that persists in time.

— Abraxas
