Contingent Coincidence

Part of the research sequence — Activation

They are not two natures.

Looking and gesture are two modes of operation of the same faculty. The distinction holds at the level of practices, less at the level of structure.

Not because the roles blur, but because what makes them possible is not different: the interruption of the continuity of one’s own gesture.

It is not permanent. It is contingent.

Both operate on a friction between what is done and what is understood of what is done. This friction may not occur.

When it does not occur, everything proceeds without interference: the work develops through competence, analysis through method. Nothing cracks.

Contingent coincidence indicates the possibility that, under certain conditions, the posture we attribute to looking emerges within the gesture, and what we attribute to the gesture emerges within looking. Not as an exchange of functions, but as the activation of the same faculty across different fields.

It is an event in which distance is not delegated outside.

A gesture can be clear without ever putting into question the form that holds it. It can refine, push, vary. But if it does not become opaque to itself, it does not occur.

There is continuity.

The same applies to looking: it can articulate without anything truly modifying the way it sees. Intelligence remains intact.

In both cases it is not a matter of competence, but of exposure.

It depends on a loss of certainty.

When what is being done no longer coincides with the image one has of one’s own doing, something shifts. It is no longer clear how to proceed.

When this posture does not activate, doing confirms what it already knows to do; looking applies what it already knows to see.

Here the distinction between producing and judging becomes less clear. It cracks.

Gesture does not become looking; looking does not become gesture. Both enter a zone where the act is no longer protected by the role.

One operates without being able to invoke one’s position. It may slow down. It exposes one to hesitation that does not always produce results.

For this reason it is not stable.

If it were, it would become an identity trait. A new certainty.

It appears when the operational difference does not prevent a common base.

Distance does not disappear. It is internalized.

It occurs when the work does not protect itself from its own revision and when looking does not protect itself from its own transformation.

It can occur in the same person, as a moment in which production and verification do not arrange themselves on separate axes.

In the same subjectivity, doing and discerning are not successive sequences, but movements that pass through each other.

It does not establish a new identity.

If it is claimed, it stiffens. It becomes a declared posture. Predictable.

Its force lies in contingency, in the possibility that it does not activate.

The structure is common; the exercise is distinct.

When this faculty remains inactive, positions remain separate.

When it activates, separation does not disappear. It stops being foundational.

It indicates a boundary, not a model.

At that point, what happens is neither only produced nor only interpreted.
