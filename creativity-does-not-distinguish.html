Creativity Does Not Distinguish

Part of the research sequence — Activation

“Creativity” does not imply radical invention. It indicates an essential capacity: to produce differences from what is already given.

For this reason, it does not distinguish. It is a diffuse faculty, transversal, operative across different fields.

Art, instead, requires a distinction.

The equivalence between art and creativity is not original. It is a construction, and as such, it can crack.

For a long time, art is not thought in terms of difference. It is structured around mimesis: not as copy, but as a regulated relation to a model.

The production of differences is not absent, but it does not constitute what distinguishes art.

Only later will this capacity be taken as a criterion, overlapping art and creativity.

The artist is not a creator, but an operator. He operates under shared constraints, in a situated practice.

He does not invent to distinguish himself, but continues to make recognizable. The value of the work does not lie in novelty, but in its capacity to hold a form.

In this regime, creativity is not absent. It is simply not pertinent.

The fracture does not occur with the individual, but with the criterion.

With the Enlightenment, the criterion is no longer guaranteed by an external order. It shifts into the subject.

This is not only a philosophical change. It is an operative condition: it becomes possible to think that the source of the rule coincides with the one who operates.

With modern aesthetics, and explicitly with Kant, this possibility takes form: genius is the one who “gives the rule to art”.

The rule no longer precedes the work, but can emerge from it.

This does not yet define the artist as creative. It introduces a possibility, not a quality.

If the rule can emerge from operating, it is no longer clear what distinguishes a gesture that institutes from one that varies.

It is in its later interpretation that this possibility is read as a property of the individual. Here the misunderstanding emerges.

Romanticism does not resolve it. It stabilizes it. It transforms a structural condition into a subjective quality: the artist becomes genius, the work expression, originality value.

What was a problem — the emergence of the rule — becomes a property of the individual.

The misunderstanding takes form: art and creativity begin to overlap.

With the Impressionists, representation loses centrality. It is no longer about returning the world, but about registering perceptual variations.

This shift follows the possibility opened by photography: the image is no longer only constructed, but can be recorded.

Perception changes, but the criterion remains tied to production.

For a real fracture to occur, it is necessary to exit this scheme.

The relation between vision and hand breaks.

At the origin, the device does not precede the problem: it is crossed by subjects already formed by a visual tension.

It does not function as an automatism, but as a displacement of a tension of research.

This does not eliminate the problem of the image. It displaces it.

It no longer concerns only how to represent, but what can be taken as image.

It is with Marcel Duchamp that the passage becomes explicit.

The ready-made does not introduce a new form. It does not modify the object. It does not intensify perception.

It intervenes elsewhere.

The gesture is not productive. It is selective.

An object is removed from its context and placed in another. It does not change in itself. What changes is the threshold that makes it readable.

Here the misunderstanding breaks.

Creativity — understood as production of differences — is no longer enough. One can produce nothing and still operate a shift.

It is no longer a necessary condition.

Art does not coincide with what is done, but with the conditions in which something can be seen as art.

With Andy Warhol, this passage moves into reproduction.

Images are no longer isolated objects, but already in circulation. Repetition does not create difference. It exposes it.

It makes visible the serial condition of the image. It does not introduce novelty. It modifies the regime of visibility.

The same problem re-emerges today with artificial intelligence.

Like photography in its early stages, it introduces a technical discontinuity: the possibility of generating images without direct manual intervention.

But here a further shift occurs: the device tends to precede the question.

The image does not arise from a necessity, but from an activated possibility. It does not emerge from a tension, but from an available option.

In these conditions, creativity operates within already given margins: it selects, combines, explores.

Production expands, while conditions remain mostly stable.

The effect of surprise derives from the system, not from a shift in the conditions of visibility.

The question is not what the medium allows to do, but what it forces to reconsider.

Without this passage, AI remains a tool of production. With this passage, it can become a place of interrogation.

But this shift does not originate with artificial intelligence. It makes it visible.

If in the artist-craftsman value was in conformity to a practice, and in the modern artist in the capacity to produce difference, today it tends to shift to the visibility of the subject.

Not what is done, but who is recognized as author.

If creativity is diffuse, accessible, automatable, what remains to distinguish is no longer the gesture, but who signs it.

The artist tends to coincide with their own visibility.

No longer only the one who operates within a threshold, but the one who occupies a recognizable position.

Not by loss of quality, but by transformation of the criterion.

Difference is no longer guaranteed by the work, but by its circulation. It is constructed, maintained, recognized.

In this condition, the initial misunderstanding completes itself: creativity — now generalized — no longer distinguishes.

And art risks being identified with its exposure.

A more fragile possibility remains.

Not in production, not in intensity, not in visibility.

But in the threshold.

A creative gesture coincides with its effect. An artistic gesture holds a distance from what it produces.

This distance is not guaranteed. It can be absorbed, neutralized, made invisible.

For this reason, the distinction between creativity and art cannot be fixed.

Not because it is uncertain, but because it is continuously put into play.

Each time a variation remains internal to what already functions, the misunderstanding reproduces itself.

Each time it modifies the conditions of functioning themselves, art reappears — without coinciding with creativity.
